MAIN IDEAS TO FOCUS ON:

Anxiety disorders are characterized by excessive fear, anxiety, and related behavioral disturbances
Generalized anxiety disorder involves persistent, uncontrollable worry about various aspects of life
Panic disorder is marked by recurrent unexpected panic attacks and fear of future attacks
Phobias are intense, irrational fears of specific objects or situations
Social anxiety disorder involves fear of social situations and potential negative evaluation
Obsessive-compulsive disorder is now classified separately but shares anxiety features
Biological, psychological, and social factors all contribute to the development of anxiety disorders
Cognitive-behavioral therapy and pharmacological treatments are commonly used to manage anxiety symptoms
Early identification and intervention can improve long-term outcomes for individuals with anxiety disorders
