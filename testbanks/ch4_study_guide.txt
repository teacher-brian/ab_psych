STUDY GUIDE: Chapter 4 – Mood Disorders

Major Ideas to Focus On:
1. Understand the diagnostic criteria and symptom profiles for major depressive disorder (MDD).
2. Distinguish between major depressive disorder, persistent depressive disorder, and premenstrual dysphoric disorder.
3. Learn the features of bipolar I and bipolar II disorders, including hypomania and mania.
4. Examine the biological, cognitive, and social risk factors for mood disorders.
5. Understand the treatment options for mood disorders including CBT, behavioral activation, and medications.
6. Review gender and cultural differences in prevalence and presentation of mood disorders.
7. Explore the comorbidity patterns and course of illness for depressive and bipolar disorders.
8. Learn the cognitive models of depression, including Beck’s cognitive triad and learned helplessness.
9. Understand the role of neurotransmitters and genetics in mood disorders.
